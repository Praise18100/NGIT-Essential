What happens behind the scenes when you press Enter:
When you type google.com and press Enter, the browser first checks the DNS to find the IP address of Googleâ€™s server. After getting the IP, your browser sends a request to that server asking for the webpage. Since the site uses HTTPS, your browser also establishes a secure, encrypted connection. The server then sends back HTML, CSS, JavaScript, images, and other files. Finally, your browser processes these files, builds the page structure, and displays the Google homepage on your screen.
